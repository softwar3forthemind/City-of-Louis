The concept of the Kingdom of Heaven (KOH) is the most important concept to mankind, as it represents God’s mind implanted into the beings of His Children, i.e., humans. Moreover, the KOH is directly related to the concept of the Rapture, which we will explore thoroughly in this letter.

While essential for our understanding of ourselves, I contend that the vast majority of people have no working definition of the phrase KOH and the term Rapture. Yes, we understand that the KOH is a pleasant place in which to reside. We understand that the KOH is a pleasant place in which to reside posthumously. 

We can imagine the KOH in our mind’s eye; however, the KOH is as much a sought after paradise as it is a term for developing and maintaining national, state, or clan security. To this end, I am here today to illustrate that the KOH has very practical applications, especially when thinking about the cyclical nature of civilization. We will begin by articulating working definitions of the KOH as well as the Rapture, which is a term that directly supports our understanding of the KOH concept.

Within the context of civilizational cycles broadly, the KOH represents existence within a God-structure, or theocracy, or government, all of which I view as synonyms; under which the citizens live in peace, security, and abundance. 

The KOH *is* the civilization. The KOH represents a thriving, unified, peaceful, secure, and wealthy civilization. 

By virtue of the collective civilization’s commitment to a given, successful God-structure, or theocratic way of being, the citizens of said civilization enjoy a Garden of Eden, or KOH, from which they can be removed only by dismantling the existing God-structure in their minds, i.e., destroying the KOH within themselves.

Taken to its logical conclusion, as touched on a moment ago, the above thinking is *an integral part of national security, or even security at a familial, clan, or municipal level.*

Similarly, on an individual basis, as I alluded to just above, the KOH represents the installation of a mindset that alters one’s character in such a way as to afford them participation in God’s heavenly plan, i.e., the holistic, evolving Garden of Eden given to humanity. 

Develop the prescribed KOH internally as an individual, and reap the fruits of the Garden of Eden with ease. Fail to develop the prescribed KOH internally, and we will likely find ourselves banished from paradise; possibly into the netherworld.

Again, these are very practical, working definitions that reveal both the nature of the concepts as well as the nature of civilization. We can see how a civilization could go from the KOH to a wasteland by way of, one by one, individual participants of the society abandoning the prescribed KOH mindset and character. 

I will now illustrate a few concrete examples for you.

In this example, God would’ve furnished to His children the KOH mentality, with which His children enjoyed ongoing existence in paradise.

So long as Adam and Eve and their 100,000 compatriots maintained the KOH, or God-structure, or Law of God, in their minds, they wouldn’t be banished. Their children and their children’s children, so long as they maintained the KOH in their minds, would likewise remain in the Garden of Eden, or the Kingdom of Heaven, eternally.

Imagine that they decided their former KOH mental schematic was insufficient.
